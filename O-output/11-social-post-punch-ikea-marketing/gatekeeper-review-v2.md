### What's Good

1. **Two-layer structure is powerful.** First half: WHAT IKEA did (RTM story). Second half: WHY it worked (psychology). This is richer than the WHOOP post because it doesn't just describe the move - it explains the human mechanics underneath.

2. **"That's not content. That's a mirror."** Best line in the post. 7 words. The Reframe pattern at its sharpest. Will get quoted and screenshotted.

3. **"An abandoned baby clinging to the only thing that gives him comfort."** This sentence does the emotional work without being sentimental. It's factual - but the feeling is inescapable. Eye-level: describes what happened, lets the reader feel it.

4. **"They didn't plan it. They recognized it."** Clean two-sentence bridge. Sets up the entire post's thesis in 9 words.

5. **IKEA across 3 countries shows the brand's speed.** Japan CEO visit, Spain catalog ad, Hong Kong social post. Three different responses, one unified instinct. Proves this wasn't one office's decision - it was brand-level reflexes.

6. **Ending is stronger than v1.** "The best brands don't create feelings. They show up when those feelings already exist." More specific, more actionable than "The best marketing isn't planned. It's recognized."
