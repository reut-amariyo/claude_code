How did a rejected baby monkey turn a $20 toy into a genius marketing move?

If this isn't a masterclass in real-time marketing, I don't know what is.

Here's the story:

A 7-month-old monkey named Punch was abandoned by his mother at a zoo in Japan.

Zookeepers gave him a $20 IKEA plush orangutan as a comfort toy. He grabbed it and wouldn't let go.

The internet couldn't look away. 30M+ views on TikTok. Global news coverage. Unprecedented crowds at the zoo.

But IKEA didn't just sit back and watch the moment pass. They moved.

IKEA donated dozens of plushies to the zoo and published this ad: "Sometimes family is who we find along the way."

No agency brief. No big budget. Just real-time marketing done right.

Here's the interesting part:

Every person who watched that story projected their own experience onto it. Loneliness. Rejection. Finding something to hold onto.

And IKEA understood that the moment wasn't about a toy. It was about a feeling.

So they showed up, made themselves part of the story, and let the emotion do the branding.

The best brands don't create feelings. They show up when those feelings already exist.
