## What Works Well

1. **Timely hook.** "The NBA All-Star game is tonight." is clean, topical, and creates immediate context. Same energy as "In 2019, Nike walked away from Amazon" — external event as entry point.
2. **Real stats are powerful.** Kobe 14,481, MJ 12,345, LeBron 15,000+. These are specific, verified, and create genuine surprise. The contrast between "greatest ever" and "most misses ever" is the engine of the post.
3. **"Nobody remembers the misses" section.** This is the emotional core. Reads naturally, not forced.
4. **"Most people I know are sitting on the bench."** Excellent eye-level framing. "Most people I know" includes Lior in the observation. Not condescending.
5. **"Not because they can't play, but because they're afraid to miss."** Uses the "but because" connector pattern. Eye-level. This is the post's best sentence.
6. **No forced tech metaphor.** The basketball parallel stays in basketball. Smart restraint.
7. **Paragraph density is right.** Follows the later-era Lior style (tighter, not 1 idea per line).

## What Needs Improvement

1. **Ending echoes a famous meme quote.** "The shots you never take are the ones that cost you" is too close to "You miss 100% of the shots you don't take" (Wayne Gretzky / Michael Scott). LinkedIn readers will catch this instantly. Needs a more original reframe.
   - **Fix:** End with a clean insight that's specific to the volume-shooting metaphor, not the "untaken shots" cliche. Or use an either/or tension question.

2. **No personal "I" grounding.** The post reads like an observation piece, but Lior's best posts have at least one personal anchor. Without it, anyone could have written this.
   - **Fix:** Add one personal line. Something like "I've launched things that missed. A lot." or connect it to his builder identity. Doesn't need to be a full story — just one line of personal stake.

3. **"But here's what nobody talks about:" is slightly cliché.** The "here's what nobody talks about" construction is overused on LinkedIn. Lior's transitions are more direct.
   - **Fix:** Try a simpler turn: "But look at the numbers:" or just cut the transition and let the stats follow the setup directly.

4. **"The reason they made those shots isn't talent. It's volume."** The "isn't X, it's Y" reframe is strong, but "talent" is debatable for NBA legends. Readers will pushback on this.
   - **Fix:** "The reason they made those shots isn't luck. It's volume." — luck is inarguable. Or soften: "The reason they made those shots? Volume."

5. **"Again and again and again."** Effective as rhythm, but a standalone line like this feels slightly staged. Owner tends to absorb these into the paragraph.
   - **Fix:** Absorb: "...they put the ball up again and again and again."

6. **User provided updated stats (top scorers' misses).** The user shared stats from Gemini: LeBron 15,300+, Kareem 12,400+, Karl Malone 12,600+. Consider using the "top 3 all-time scorers" framing since the user specifically referenced this. The current draft uses Kobe/MJ/LeBron which is more iconic but doesn't match the user's latest input.
   - **Fix:** Owner decision — keep Kobe/MJ/LeBron (more iconic) or switch to LeBron/Kareem/Malone (top scorers angle). Recommend keeping Kobe/MJ/LeBron because they're more universally recognized and emotionally resonant.

## Minor Observations (Not Blocking)

1. "30 of the best players in the world" — the new format is USA vs. World with round-robin games. May want to just say "the best players in the world" without the specific count.
2. "on one court" — technically it's a round-robin tournament now. Minor, but nitpick.
3. Consider if "tonight" is accurate — the All-Star game is Feb 16 (Sunday). If posting on Feb 15 (Saturday), "tomorrow" is more accurate. If posting Sunday, "tonight" works.
