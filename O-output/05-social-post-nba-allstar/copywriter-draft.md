# Social Post: NBA All-Star - Start Playing

**Date:** 2026-02-15
**Version:** v1
**Platform:** LinkedIn

---

## The Post

The NBA All-Star game is tonight. 30 of the best players in the world, on one court.
But here's what nobody talks about:
Kobe Bryant missed 14,481 shots in his career. More than any player in NBA history at the time. Michael Jordan missed 12,345. LeBron just broke the all-time record with over 15,000 missed shots.
These are the greatest to ever play the game. And they missed more than most players even attempted.
Nobody remembers the misses. Nobody replays the airballs at 2am. What people remember is the shot that went in when it mattered.
The reason they made those shots isn't talent. It's volume. They kept shooting. Every single game, every single quarter, they put the ball up. Again and again and again.
Most people I know are sitting on the bench. Not because they can't play, but because they're afraid to miss. They're waiting for the perfect shot, the perfect moment, the perfect conditions.
That moment doesn't exist.
The best players in the world miss more than half their shots. And they're celebrated for it. You don't need to have the perfect idea, the perfect product, or the perfect pitch. You just need to start shooting. The misses don't matter. The shots you never take are the ones that cost you.

## Copywriter Notes

### Choices I Made

**Archetype:** Scene-Based Lesson (external event as entry point, like Nike-Amazon Sample 05) crossed with Empathy-First Framework (like Gold Standard Sample 07)

**Hook:** "The NBA All-Star game is tonight." - Statement, timely, one-liner. Uses the external event as the entry point (same technique as "In 2019, Nike walked away from Amazon" and "Shopify just removed over 10,000 of our reviews"). Topical hook that leverages a cultural moment.

**Numbers:** All real stats - Kobe 14,481, MJ 12,345, LeBron 15,000+. User asked for "a number that feels real" - these ARE real. The contrast between "greatest ever" and "most misses ever" is the tension engine.

**Transition:** "But here's what nobody talks about:" - uses the "But" turn technique from voice-dna (like "But WHOOP didn't stop there"). Also uses colon as reveal device.

**Eye-level tone:** "Most people I know are sitting on the bench. Not because they can't play, but because they're afraid to miss." Uses the "but because" connector pattern. Empathizes rather than lectures. The "most people I know" framing includes the writer in the observation - eye-level, not from above.

**Ending:** "The misses don't matter. The shots you never take are the ones that cost you." - Clean landing. The insight IS the ending. No forced mic-drop, no question. Echoes the post's theme without restating it. Follows Sample 07 pattern where the last line delivers the reframe.

**Kept from user's brief:**
- Famous players who missed thousands of shots (used real stats)
- Nobody remembers what they missed, only what they made
- Reminder to start playing / taking action

**Removed/avoided:**
- No bold on any headers or key phrases within the body
- No forced tech metaphor connecting basketball to startups
- No "I" personal story (this post is observation-based, like Nike-Amazon)
- No numbered list (doesn't fit this narrative arc)
- No hashtags

**Tone decisions:**
- "Most people I know" instead of "you" for the fear observation - less accusatory, more shared
- "They kept shooting" - simple, not dramatized
- No profanity needed - the stats speak for themselves
- Present tense throughout ("is tonight," "people remember," "you don't need")

**Length:** ~200 words. Medium. Good for a story-observation post.

### Alternatives Considered

**Alt hook:** "Kobe Bryant missed 14,481 shots." - Stronger as a shock stat hook, but the user specifically asked for the All-Star context, and starting with the event creates timeliness.

**Alt ending:** "So what's your excuse for not shooting?" - Decided against it: this sounds condescending. Fails the eye-level tone test. "Could this be read as 'I'm better than you'?" - Yes. Cut.

**Alt ending 2:** "Are you waiting for the perfect shot, or are you going to start playing?" - Either/or tension question. Valid option. Decided the clean landing reframe was stronger here because the whole post already asks this question implicitly.

**Alt structure:** Considered adding a personal "I" example - "I've launched products that flopped. I've pitched investors who said no." But the user's brief was specifically about NBA/basketball as the metaphor. Adding a personal anecdote would dilute the basketball frame.

**Alt opening with "I":** "I'm watching the NBA All-Star game tonight." - Decided against: the post isn't about Lior watching basketball. It's about the lesson from the stats. Starting with the event is cleaner.
