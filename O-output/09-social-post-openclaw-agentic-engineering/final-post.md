No team. No code. Just a conversation with an agent.

That's how Peter Steinberger built OpenClaw and broke GitHub records.

I just spent 3 hours listening to the man behind one of the most controversial open-source AI projects, and here's what stayed with me:

1. Peter sent a voice message to his agent on WhatsApp by accident. The agent had no voice support. So it figured out how to convert the audio, transcribe it, and reply on its own. He never taught it how to do any of that.

2. He wrote a personal file for his agent - who he is, how he thinks, what matters to him. The agent started adapting on its own. After Peter had shoulder surgery, the agent sent him a message in the hospital asking if he was okay. Peter never told it about the surgery. It just knew.

3. Peter watched his own agent click the "I'm not a bot" checkbox on his screen. A bot, proving it's not a bot. And it passed.

We're only getting started.

If you're building with AI agents, here's what to take from this:

Treat them like junior employees, not tools.

Stop over-instructing. The WhatsApp story happened because Peter didn't limit what the agent could try. Give it a goal, not a script.

Keep your prompts short. The best builders go through a phase of over-engineering everything. Then they come back to simple and direct.
