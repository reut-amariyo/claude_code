# Social Post: Lior Pozin - Zero Ego in Business

**Date:** 2026-02-12
**Version:** v1
**Platform:** LinkedIn
**Client:** Lior Pozin

---

## The Post

At 21, I thought I knew everything about dropshipping.

Then DSM-Tool (the market leader) started beating us. Two brothers, MBAs, connections, money. I had fear and an internal "no."

Here's what I did: I watched them. Learned from them. Took their best ideas and made them better.

5 years later, we overtook them.

Then we acquired them.

Zero ego in learning. Massive ego in execution.

When you're building, competitors aren't threats. They're your R&D team. They test ideas, you take what works and improve it.

The founders who lose are the ones who think they're too good to learn.

The founders who win treat every competitor like a free consulting session.

I've acquired 4 competitors. Every single one taught me something before the deal closed.

What's the last thing you learned from a competitor?

## Copywriter Notes

### Choices I Made

**Hook Strategy:**
- One-liner bold statement (per Lior's LinkedIn rules)
- "At 21, I thought I knew everything" creates immediate tension and relatability
- Sets up the ego → no-ego transformation

**3-Line Outline (LinkedIn Rule #2):**
Lines 1-3 outline the full post:
- Line 1: Young, overconfident
- Line 2: Market leader beating us
- Line 3: What I did (the pivot)

**Story Selection:**
- Used DSM-Tool acquisition story from Lior-Prompt.md
- Real competitor, real fear, real outcome (acquisition)
- Demonstrates zero ego → massive results pattern

**Lior's Voice Markers:**
- Specific age: "At 21"
- Real company name: "DSM-Tool"
- Concrete outcome: "5 years later," "acquired them"
- Bold statement: "Zero ego in learning. Massive ego in execution."
- Direct language: "watched them," "took their best ideas"

**Signposting (LinkedIn Rule #4):**
- "I've acquired 4 competitors" (establishes authority)

**Formatting:**
- No double dashes (used periods instead)
- No rocket emojis
- Ultra-short paragraphs (1-3 lines)
- Power sentences: "Zero ego in learning. Massive ego in execution." (7 words)
- Ends with question (LinkedIn Rule #5)

**Structural Alignment:**
- Hook (line 1)
- Context (DSM-Tool setup)
- Action ("I watched them...")
- Outcome ("overtook them," "acquired them")
- Insight ("Zero ego in learning...")
- Framework ("competitors = R&D team")
- Lesson ("founders who win...")
- Signposting ("I've acquired 4...")
- CTA question

**ICP Targeting:**
- Speaks to bootstrapped founders facing bigger competitors
- Addresses imposter syndrome and competitive fear
- Shows vulnerability (fear, "internal no") then victory
- Actionable principle they can apply today

### Alternatives I Considered

**Alternative Hook 1:**
"I used to fear my competitors. Now I acquire them."
→ Rejected: Too arrogant, doesn't show the transformation

**Alternative Hook 2:**
"Want to know how I turned my biggest fear into a $X acquisition?"
→ Rejected: Clickbaity, doesn't match Lior's direct style

**Alternative Story:**
Could have used the $30K coach loan story or PE walkaway
→ Rejected: DSM-Tool story is perfect for "zero ego" theme (learning from competition)

**Alternative Ending:**
"Who's your biggest competitor? What can you learn from them today?"
→ Rejected: Current ending is more open-ended and inclusive
