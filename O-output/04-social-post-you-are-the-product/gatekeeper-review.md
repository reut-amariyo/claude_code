## What Works Well

1. **Hook executes the gold standard pattern.** Validates what reader does ("You spend weeks on your product roadmap") then pivots to the gap ("But when was the last time you put that energy into yourself?"). Same architecture as "You love tech. You really do."
2. **"Things never calm down."** Four words. Punch line. The reader feels it because it's true.
3. **Concrete delegation examples.** "I stopped editing decks, reviewing minor expenses, and scheduling my own meetings" - visual, specific, relatable. Not abstract "delegate more."
4. **"Most performance problems aren't motivation problems. They're infrastructure problems."** Strong reframe. Quotable. Shareable.
5. **Pronoun precision correct.** "I" for personal opinions, concrete actions. "You" for reader address. No passive.
6. **Ending works.** "Why are you running yourself without one?" - rhetorical, direct, forces the reader to answer internally.
7. **No stacked metaphors.** Original had 4 tech metaphors in a row. This uses the product/roadmap parallel once as a frame, then stays grounded.
