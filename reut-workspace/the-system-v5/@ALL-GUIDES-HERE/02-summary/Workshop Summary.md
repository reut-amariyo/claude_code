## The ABC-TOM System (One Page, Keep Forever)

> "Set up ABC once. Run TOM daily."

### ABC = What You Set Up

| | What It Is | What's Inside | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** = Agents | Your team | Copywriter, Gatekeeper, Tom... | No agents, no one to execute |
| **B** = Brain | The brain | Writing samples, research, knowledge, INBOX for quick capture | No knowledge, the agents are guessing |
| **C** = Core | The core | Who you are, how you sound, who you write for | No core, everything is generic |

### TOM = What You Run Daily

| | What It Is | What's Inside | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| **T** = Tools | Toolbox | Skills, prompts, workflows | Tools save time |
| **O** = Output | Output | Everything created, organized in folders | Order = sanity |
| **M** = Memory | Memory | What worked, what didn't, decisions | This is what makes the system improve |

### The Loop = What Makes It Compound

| From | To | What Happens |
|---|---|---|
| **M-memory** | **B-brain** | Best learnings become knowledge |
| **M-memory** | **C-core** | Recurring patterns become rules |

**Set up ABC once. Run TOM daily. Close The Loop to keep improving.**

                    |

Notice what's happening here. You're not writing. You're not editing. You're managing. And the system gets smarter every round.

## 8 Things Worth Knowing

**1. Be specific.**
"Write me a post" is mediocre. "Write me a post about why business owners are afraid to give up control, for an audience of entrepreneurs aged 30-45, in a personal and honest tone" is excellent. The more precise you are, the more precise the agents are.

**2. Give examples.**
Want a specific style? Paste an example and say "like this." Agents learn from seeing, not from explaining.

**3. Trust the Gatekeeper.**
It's annoying sometimes. But it keeps your standards high, and that's the whole point. Let it do its job.

**4. Give specific feedback.**
"This isn't good" helps no one. "The opening is weak, I want to open with a personal story like in sample 01" helps a lot. Specific > generic. Always.

**5. Update the files.**
`voice-dna.md` isn't set in stone. You change, your writing changes, your audience changes. The files need to change with you.

**6. New project? Duplicate the folder.**
Want to write a book? Work with a client? Side project? Duplicate the-system, rename it, start filling. Just make sure the folder lives inside your Obsidian Vault.

**7. Update the memory and close The Loop.**
After every project, write in `M-memory/learning-log.md` what worked and what didn't. This is the single most important thing in the whole system. It's what turns it from good to great. Then periodically run prompt 14 to promote your best insights back into Brain and Core. That's The Loop. That's where the real compounding happens. (You can also ask Claude to update memory himself.)
