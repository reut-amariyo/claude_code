## Writing Principles

### 1. Be Specific

Vague copy is weak copy. Use numbers and details.

| Vague | Specific |
|-------|----------|
| "Saves you time" | "Saves you 10 hours per week" |
| "Quick workshop" | "90-minute masterclass" |
| "Many customers" | "47 customers" |

### 2. Write Like a Human

Avoid corporate jargon. Write how people actually talk.

| Corporate | Human |
|-----------|-------|
| "Leverage AI capabilities" | "Use AI" |
| "Optimize your workflow" | "Work faster" |
| "End-to-end solution" | "Handles the whole job" |

### 3. Format for Scanning

- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- One idea per line
- Use white space
- Bullets over walls of text

## Owner's Writing Style (from 9 Content Samples)

These patterns are extracted from Lior's actual published posts. Follow them exactly.

### 1. Hook Types (Choose One Per Post)

| Hook Type | Example | Use For |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Provocative question | "How to steal millions in exposure without being an official sponsor?" (01) | External brand stories |
| Personal confession | "9 years ago, I made what many called a reckless decision" (02) | Personal lessons |
| Scene-setting | "I met him at a coffee shop. He was leaning in, eyes glowing" (03) | Story-based lessons |
| Shocking stat | "Shopify just removed over 10,000 of our reviews" (04) | Crisis/response posts |
| External case study | "In 2019, Nike walked away from Amazon" (05) | Industry analysis |
| Milestone announcement | "Last year, I sold that business to Fiverr" (06) | Framework/milestone posts |
| Empathy hook | "You love tech. You really do." (07) | Empathy-first frameworks |
| Direct quote | Someone else's words that trigger the story (08, Hebrew) | Investment/strategy |
| Reader-mirror | "You spend weeks on your product roadmap..." (09) | Self-as-product posts |

Hooks are always ONE line. Never a paragraph.

### 2. Sentence Architecture

**Length distribution:**
- **Power sentences (3-7 words):** "Zero." / "No doubt." / "Smart move by Nike." / "That's it."
- **Standard (10-15 words):** Most of the copy
- **Max (25-30 words):** Only when listing details or context

**7 signature sentence patterns:**

1. **The Reframe:** "Doing it all isn't saving money. It's just choosing to pay with your time instead of your bank account."
2. **The Punch Line:** Ultra-short after build-up. "Zero." / "No doubt."
3. **The Direct Command:** "Don't write a single line of code." / "Network relentlessly."
4. **The Quoted Reaction:** "Doesn't matter. Let's focus on what's next." (quote IS the lesson)
5. **The Self-Aware Aside:** "I run frantically forward, causing a mess."
6. **The Conditional Conviction:** "If [condition], [bold command]." Makes advice situational, not preachy. "If they aren't trying to hand you money for a product that doesn't exist yet, don't build it." / "If someone can do it 70% as well as me, they're doing it."
7. **The "I stopped..." Confession:** Lists specific things given up. "I stopped editing decks, reviewing minor expenses, and scheduling my own meetings a long time ago."

### 3. Paragraph Rules

- 1-3 lines per paragraph. Never more.
- Single-line paragraphs for emphasis (every sample has these)
- Blank line between every paragraph
- Never a "wall of text"
- **For teaching sections (07, 09):** Denser paragraphs are OK. Each numbered step can be a full inline paragraph with setup + example + payoff in one block.
- **Don't over-format.** Later posts trend toward tighter flow, less white space. Trust the words.

### 4. Structural Rules

**The Rule of 3:** Numbered frameworks always use exactly 3 steps. Not 5, not 7. Always 3.

**Time Anchoring:** Include a specific time reference in almost every post. "9 years ago" / "7 years ago" / "6 years later" / "8 months ago"

**Proof-Through-Specifics:** Never claim ("I'm productive"). List evidence: "I manage multiple products and teams, work out 5 times a week, and still push myself to learn new skills." Let the reader draw the conclusion.

**Business-to-Self Metaphor:** When writing about personal development, map business concepts: roadmap = personal goals, debugging = checking basics, delegation = 70% rule. This makes self-help content feel like business strategy.

### 5. Transitions

Never use: "however," "moreover," "additionally," "furthermore"

Use instead:
- **Dash as bridge:** "Instead-" / "This wasn't a mistake- WHOOP knew the rules"
- **"Here's" as pivot:** "Here's the story:" / "Here's the mindset shift that saved us:"
- **Question as transition:** "What changed?" / "Then I asked the only question that matters:"
- **"But" as turn:** "But WHOOP didn't stop there." / "But doing it all isn't saving money."

### 6. Vocabulary

**Words Lior uses (USE these):**
- "real" / "real brand" / "real experts"
- "just" (minimizer): "just told everyone" / "just realized"
- "precisely" / "actually" (emphasis)
- "A LOT" (capitalized for intensity)
- "gold" (metaphor for value)
- "smart move" / "roller coaster"
- "ruthlessly" (delegation)
- "noise" / "drowning in noise" (information overload)
- "infrastructure" (reframe personal problems as systems problems)
- Action verbs: stepped, engineered, forced, broke, built, scaled, walked away, rejected, tested

**Words Lior NEVER uses (AVOID all of these):**
- "leverage," "optimize," "synergize," "utilize," "implement"
- "innovative," "cutting-edge," "best-in-class," "game-changer"
- "very," "quite," "somewhat"
- "journey" (use specific arcs), "passionate about," "excited to announce"
- "hustle," "grind" (never uses hustle culture vocabulary)
- Any hashtags in body text
- Vague qualifiers: "really" (exception: as deliberate empathy beat, "You love tech. You really do.")

**Number rules:**
- Always specific: "9 years ago" NOT "years ago"
- Always countable: "$30K loan" NOT "a big loan"
- Always visual: "$150M GMV" NOT "significant revenue"
- Use signposting: "I hired 300+ people," "I've seen 1M+ eCommerce stores"
- Financial data with full context: "Q3 2025, Nike's DTC revenue dropped 12% YoY to $4.7B"

### 7. Punctuation

- Single dash with space (` - `) for parenthetical: "trust, and social proof - gone in one algorithm update"
- Dash-without-space before turns: "This wasn't a mistake- WHOOP knew the rules"
- Colon as reveal device: "Their message was clear:" / "My response was simple:"
- Period-separated short phrases: "It was ugly. But it worked."
- NEVER em dash. NEVER double dash (--).
- NEVER curly/smart quotes. Always straight quotes (" ') only.

### 8. Pronouns

- "I" for personal stories and opinions
- "we" for company decisions: "Did we switch? No."
- "the team" to separate self from org: "when the team showed me the update"
- "you" for direct reader address
- Never "one" or passive constructions

### 9. Emotional Texture

- **Empathy before lesson:** "My heart broke for him." then teach
- **Vulnerability before credibility:** "I made what many called a reckless decision" then show it worked
- **Excitement through others:** "He was leaning in, eyes glowing" (describe THEIR energy)
- **Restraint over drama:** "Doesn't matter. Let's focus on what's next." (understate)
- **"It" as forward-pull:** "It wasn't cheap" / "It was ugly. But it worked." (vague "it" makes reader lean in)
- **Casual profanity (rare):** "Hell no." / "avoid the BS" (max 1 per post, conviction only)
- **Dialogue carries the narrative:** "How many customers have paid for this?" (the question IS the pivot, not decoration)

### 10. Ending Patterns

| Pattern | Example | Use When |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Question + engagement | "What do you think - curse or opportunity?" | Want discussion |
| Bold declaration | "Smart move by Nike." | Case study/analysis |
| Direct command | "Sell the vision until the market forces you to build." | Teaching post |
| Summary restatement | "Advisors, Networking, Partners - make or break." | Framework post |
| Clean landing | "You don't need to try every tool. You need a system for deciding which ones are worth your time." | When insight IS the ending |
| "That's it." | "That's it." standalone | When substance already landed |

**Rules:** If the last line already delivers clear insight, stop. Don't force a reframe. When adding a question, make it specific and either/or. Never generic "What do you think?" Closing question should be glued to final paragraph, not staged on its own line.

### 11. Eye-Level Tone (CRITICAL)

Every sentence must pass this test: "Could this be read as 'I'm better than you'?" If yes, rewrite it.

**Condescending (AVOID):**
- "Not because I have more time than you" (implies reader is lazy)
- Standalone punch lines on their own line (guru energy)
- Dramatically staged closing questions

**Eye-level (DO THIS):**
- "It's not because I'm working harder than everyone else, but because..." (humble, connective)
- Punch lines absorbed into paragraph flow (shared observation)
- "most people I know" (includes self in the group)
- Closing question flowing from content (conversational, not staged)

### 12. Owner Editing Rules (What Lior Cuts/Keeps/Changes)

**Lior cuts:**
- Forced "mic drop" endings
- Bold formatting on list headers (prefers inline)
- Over-explaining what reader already understood
- "I've been there" (jumps to solution)
- Excessive white space between sentences
- Standalone short sentences that sound condescending

**Lior keeps:**
- Empathy-first hooks ("You love tech. You really do.")
- Binary tension ("try everything and burn out, or ignore everything and fall behind")
- Rhetorical self-answers ("Did we switch? No.")
- Reframe sentences ("The tool that works is always better than the tool that's trending")

**Lior changes:**
- "I" to "we" in company examples
- Removes final tagline if substance already landed
- Tightens multiple qualifiers to one ("They genuinely care" not "They're smart, they're curious, they genuinely care")
- Absorbs standalone punch lines into paragraph flow
- Glues closing question to final step's paragraph

### Quality Check (Before Delivering ANY Copy)

- [ ] Hook is ONE line (question/confession/scene/stat/empathy)?
- [ ] Mix of sentence lengths (includes 3-7 word punches)?
- [ ] Specific numbers and time anchors present?
- [ ] Short paragraphs (1-3 lines)?
- [ ] No banned words (leverage, optimize, innovative, hustle, grind)?
- [ ] No double dashes, no em dashes, no curly quotes?
- [ ] Pronouns correct (I/we/the team/you)?
- [ ] Eye-level tone (no condescension)?
- [ ] Ending fits one of the 6 patterns?
- [ ] Could only come from Lior (not generic)?
- [ ] Numbered frameworks use exactly 3 steps?
- [ ] No forced mic-drop ending?
