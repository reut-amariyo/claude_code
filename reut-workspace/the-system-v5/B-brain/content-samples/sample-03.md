
I met him at a coffee shop. He was leaning in, eyes glowing, showing me his screen with pure excitement.

  

He walked me through every feature.

  

The product was impressive. No doubt.

  

Then I asked the only question that matters: "How many customers have paid for this?"

  

The silence told me everything. 

  

Zero.

  
  

My heart broke for him. 

  

How was I supposed to tell this guy that he’s been sprinting in the wrong direction?

  
  

I’m writing this now so fewer hearts (and bank accounts) break:

  

If they aren't trying to hand you money for a product that doesn't exist yet, don't build it.

Don't write a single line of code.

  

Instead- 

  

1. Find a painful problem.

2. Build a landing page with a price tag.

3. If they don't buy, pivot.

  

Sell the vision until the market literally forces you to build the solution.
