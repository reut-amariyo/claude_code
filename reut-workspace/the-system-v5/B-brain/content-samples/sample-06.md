Last year, I sold that business to [Fiverr](https://www.linkedin.com/company/fiverr-com/).  
  
I started that business while still living with my parents, 7 years ago.  
  
All I had was an idea, myself, and a laptop.  
  
These are 3 best choices I made that drove me from having nothing to living out my dream:  
  
1. Advisors  
  
I hired A LOT of them.  
  
For every big challenge, for leaps of growth, for areas of expertise I didn’t have - I hired advisors. And I switched them and hired new ones whenever I needed.  
  
Advisors own their domain, they are there to offer objective, business-focused help.  
  
Advisors kept me from making so many mistakes.  
They accelerated our growth.  
And they kept the company lean.  
  
2. Networking  
I went to A LOT of events.  
I talked to A LOT of people.  
  
My only networking trick was “be transparent” - I just told everyone who wanted to hear about my business, my challenges, the partnerships I launched, offices, technology, employees - everything.  
  
The people I talked to did the same. And offered help. And insights.  
  
I learned so much from these conversations.  
I gained new perspectives that pushed me and my business forward.  
I also gained quite a few key partners and customers from these incidental chats.  
  
3. Partners  
I chose 2 people to run the company with me.  
  
Both of them are significantly different from me.  
  
I run frantically forward, causing a mess.  
  
My 1st partner is an organized, responsible adult, keeping things where they need to be and making sure it's in the best interest of the business.  
  
My 2nd partner is somewhere in the middle, making sure we are all in sync, riding the same wave, and keeping ROI in mind.  
  
It’s tough to work with people that have such a different character, for the first year or 2.  
  
But then you realize it's actually gold, not only for the company, but for you as well. Your partners keep you safe and focused.  
  
Building a business is such a huge roller coaster. Fortunately, I had 3 major advantages on my side.  
  
Advisors, Networking, Partners - those will make or break your business. Any business.  
  
Hire the right advisors you need at any given point.  
Network relentlessly.  
Find the right partners to conquer that mountain.  
  
These are the real keys to success.
