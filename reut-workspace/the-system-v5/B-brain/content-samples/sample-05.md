n 2019, [Nike](https://www.linkedin.com/company/nike/) walked away from [Amazon](https://www.linkedin.com/company/amazon/)  
  
They had a good reason to do it - they went all-in on DTC. Nike wanted to completely own the customer relationship and the profit margins.  
  
6 years later, they’re going back to Amazon.  
  
What changed?  
1. 𝐑𝐞𝐬𝐞𝐥𝐥𝐞𝐫𝐬 - Nike itself left Amazon, but resellers are still selling it on Amazon. Which means Nike is basically 𝒍𝒐𝒔𝒊𝒏𝒈 a huge playground to small players it can’t control.  
  
2. 𝐀𝐦𝐚𝐳𝐨𝐧 - it got even bigger. More than 50% of U.S. product searches now start on Amazon. Not being there was like 𝒏𝒐𝒕 𝒆𝒙𝒊𝒔𝒕𝒊𝒏𝒈 in a major part of the internet’s mall.  
  
3. 𝐑𝐞𝐯𝐞𝐧𝐮𝐞 - In Q3 2025, Nike’s DTC revenue dropped 12% YoY to $4.7B.  
Digital sales fell 15%.  
12-month Revenue is now $47.8B - 𝒂 7% 𝒅𝒆𝒄𝒍𝒊𝒏𝒆 from FY 2024 ($51.4B).  
Plus, their stock is down.  
  
It only took 6 years and a big drop in revenue to make this decision.  
  
Nike isn’t giving up on DTC. Hell no. It just realized they can’t completely control distribution.  
  
DTC can co-exist with marketplaces. It should.  
  
Smart move by Nike.
