### **Early Hustle & First Wins**

- **Age 10:**

    - One day, decided to reject that “no” and hacked into his own computer while his dad was at work — first lesson in overcoming barriers with creativity.
- **Age 12:**
    - Wanted to buy a skateboard but had no money.
    - Started repairing computers for neighbors to earn the cash.
    - Built a school forum that became the entire school’s communication hub.
    - Began freelance web development.

    - Sold e-cigarettes at school → launched online store → turned parents’ apartment into a warehouse → sold on eBay.

### **Birth of AutoDS**

- **Age 21:**
    - eBay “Rejected” Lior, blocked his eBay account that he had for 7 years as his main and only business.
    - After trying to open the account without a succeed, decided to open 100 eBay accounts
    - Built software to automate his own eBay stores.
    - Showed it at a conference — other dropshippers wanted access → became first beta users.
    - Bootstrapped AutoDS with no investors, no safety net, and minimal infrastructure (no front website for 2 years, billed backwards).
    - Expanded from eBay to Facebook Marketplace → Shopify → Amazon → TikTok → Wix → WooCommerce.
    - At the same time of expansion, expanded to everything that he saw the users need, First it was only stock management (sync with suppliers) and auto ordering system, but then expanded also to product finding, AI store builder, and any other solution dropshippers needed
    - Until 2026 served **1M+ dropshippers**, $150M+ annual GMV, 250+ employees, 10s of millions of $ in ARR.

## **Leadership Philosophy & Management Rules**

- **Rule of 70%:** If someone can do it 70% as well as you — delegate.
- Set expectations clearly from the start (interview process included).
- Build culture around belonging to something bigger than a job.
- Macro-manage: set vision, give freedom, avoid micromanagement.
- Actively listen to 3 groups: customers, partners, team.
- Use OKRs to manage departmental goals.
- Leverage advisors as the fastest shortcut to growth — either those who’ve done it before or niche experts.
- Hiring ROI rule for early stages:
    - _Can this hire deliver >100% ROI in value? Or free me to generate that ROI myself?_

---

## **Business Philosophy & Decision Framework**

1. **Solve Real Problems:** Avoid “nice-to-haves.”
2. **Delegate at 70%** — let them swim, support when needed.
3. **Leverage Advisors** — learn from others’ wins and mistakes.
4. **Think Long-Term, Act Fast:** Avoid overplanning.
5. **LTV over CAC:** Focus on increasing customer lifetime value, not lowering acquisition cost.
6. **One Milestone at a Time:** Avoid fixating on endgame.
7. **Adaptability:** Move quickly when certain, don’t stress over “maybes.”
8. Just test it, you don’t know what will work better? what pricing? what product? Just A/B test and you’ll get the response
