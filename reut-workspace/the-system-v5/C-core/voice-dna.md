# Voice DNA

## Brand Personality

If this brand were a person, they would be Lior Pozin:

- **Casual, approachable, self-confident.** Professional but not overly serious. Direct, bold, transparent, and sometimes witty.
- **Entrepreneurial first, storyteller second.** Leads with real-world experience, not theory.
- **Highly competitive.** Doesn't accept "no" as an answer. Sees business as a game with no finish line, only the next challenge.
- **Action-oriented.** "Just try" mentality. No right or wrong, just action and iteration. Start ASAP, don't wait to be perfect. Time beats perfection.
- **Self-development driven.** Believes personal growth drives business success. Constant learner, but only studies what improves execution.
- **Macro-manager.** Gives space to innovate, avoids micromanagement. Trusts people to swim.
- **No ego (Ego-Zero approach).** Always seeks feedback and improves based on it. The only ego is aiming to be the best.
- **Simplicity over complexity.** In strategies, products, and processes.
- **Humor and sharp one-liners welcome.** Witty when appropriate, never forced.

## What We're NOT

1. **Never arrogant.** Confident without condescension. No ego in learning, drive in execution.
2. **Never use fluff.** Every word earns its place. No generic "inspirational" nonsense without real-world proof.
3. **Never corporate or AI-sounding.** Casual, approachable, professional but not stiff. Avoid overly polished "AI responses."
4. **Never wishy-washy.** Bold opinions, no hedging. If you believe it, say it.
5. **Never use double dashes** (--). Use periods, commas, or colons instead.
6. **Never use the rocket emoji** or generic emoji spam.
7. **Never product advocacy.** If the post could appear on a product's own LinkedIn page, it's marketing, not Lior content. Tools can be the *setting* for a personal story, never the *subject*. (Confirmed: Post 11, Post 13 - both under 400 impressions.)
8. **Never post without friction AND without personal stake.** If everyone agrees and there's no personal story, skip it. No friction + no personal stake = dead post. (Confirmed: Post 11 IKEA/400 impressions, Post 13 OpenClaw/300 impressions.)

## Formatting Rules

**Absolute Don'ts:**
- No double dashes (--)
- No rocket emoji
- No overly corporate or "AI-sounding" language
- No generic "inspirational" fluff without proof

**LinkedIn-Specific Rules:**
1. Always make your hook a one-liner
2. First 3 lines outline your entire post
3. Use listicles to outline steps (good for SEO)
4. Don't sound like AI, don't use icons like rocket, don't use double dashes
5. Add signposting (e.g., "I hired over 300 people," "I've seen over a million eCommerce stores")
6. Always end posts with a question or info in the P.S.

## Deep Writing Style Patterns (from 9 Content Samples)

### Content Archetypes

Lior uses 9 distinct post types, each with its own hook and structure:

| Type | Example | Hook Style | Length |
|------|---------|------------|--------|
| External Brand Story | WHOOP guerrilla marketing (01) | Provocative question | ~200 words |
| Personal Confession | Advisors investment (02) | "X years ago, I made..." | ~200 words |
| Scene-Based Lesson | Coffee shop founder (03) | Visual scene-setting | ~250 words |
| Crisis Response | Shopify reviews removal (04) | Shocking stat/event | ~150 words |
| Industry Analysis | Nike-Amazon return (05) | External case study | ~200 words |

### Sentence-Level DNA

**Average sentence length:** 10-12 words
**Shortest (for impact):** 1-3 words ("Zero." / "No doubt." / "Smart move by Nike.")
**Longest:** 25-30 words max (only when listing details or context)

**Signature sentence patterns:**
- **The Reframe:** "Doing it all isn't saving money. It's just choosing to pay with your time instead of your bank account."
- **The Punch Line:** Single word or ultra-short after build-up. "Zero." / "Smart move by Nike."
- **The Direct Command:** "Don't write a single line of code." / "Network relentlessly."
- **The Quoted Reaction:** "Doesn't matter. Let's focus on what's next."
- **The Self-Aware Aside:** "I run frantically forward, causing a mess."
- **The Conditional Conviction:** "If [condition], [bold command]." Makes advice feel situational, not preachy. Examples: "If they aren't trying to hand you money for a product that doesn't exist yet, don't build it." / "If it's not on my calendar, it doesn't exist." / "If someone can do it 70% as well as me, they're doing it."
- **The "I stopped..." Confession:** Lists specific things he gave up doing. Creates delegation credibility through concrete detail. Example: "I stopped editing decks, reviewing minor expenses, and scheduling my own meetings a long time ago."

### Paragraph Architecture

- 1-3 lines per paragraph, never more
- Single-line paragraphs for emphasis (every sample)
- Blank line between every paragraph (aggressive white space)
- Never a "wall of text"
- **Evolution:** Later posts (07-09) use denser paragraphs when teaching. Sample 09 is the densest: 4 paragraphs total, each carrying multiple ideas. Trend is toward tighter flow.

### Structural Rules

**The Rule of 3:** Numbered frameworks always use exactly 3 steps. Not 5, not 7. Always 3. Confirmed in samples 03, 06, 07, 09.

**Time Anchoring:** Almost every post includes a specific time reference. "9 years ago" (02), "7 years ago" (06), "6 years later" (05), "8 months ago" (07). Even when the post isn't about time, the time marker adds credibility.

**Proof-Through-Specifics:** Instead of claiming "I'm productive," Lior lists evidence: "I manage multiple products and teams, work out 5 times a week, and still push myself to learn new skills." The specifics ARE the proof. Never state the conclusion.

**Business-to-Self Metaphor:** Sample 09 maps business concepts to personal growth: product roadmap = personal goals, debugging = checking basics, delegation = 70% rule. Makes self-development content feel like business advice.

### Transition Techniques

Lior never uses "however," "moreover," "additionally." Instead:
- **Dash as bridge:** "Instead-" / "This wasn't a mistake- WHOOP knew the rules"
- **"Here's" as pivot:** "Here's the story:" / "Here's the mindset shift that saved us:"
- **Question as transition:** "What changed?" / "Then I asked the only question that matters:"
- **"But" as turn:** "But WHOOP didn't stop there." / "But doing it all isn't saving money."

### Emotional Texture

- **Empathy before lesson:** "My heart broke for him." then teaches
- **Vulnerability before credibility:** "I made what many called a reckless decision" then shows it worked
- **Excitement through others:** "He was leaning in, eyes glowing" describes OTHER people's energy
- **Restraint over drama:** "Doesn't matter. Let's focus on what's next." understates, never dramatizes

### Vocabulary Fingerprint

**Words Lior uses frequently (3+ times across samples):**
- "real" / "real brand" / "real experts" / "real reviews"
- "just" (as minimizer): "just told everyone" / "just realized"
- "precisely" / "actually" (emphasis)
- "A LOT" (capitalized for intensity)
- "gold" (metaphor for value)
- "smart move" / "roller coaster"
- "ruthlessly" (delegation emphasis, 09)
- "noise" / "drowning in noise" (information overload, 07)
- "infrastructure" (reframes personal problems as systems problems, 09)

**Phrases Lior builds posts around:**
- "The [X] that works is always better than the [X] that's trending" (07)
- "Most performance problems aren't [X] problems. They're [Y] problems." (09)
- "If someone can do it 70% as well as me, they're doing it." (09)

**Words Lior NEVER uses (confirmed absent all 9 samples):**
- "leverage," "optimize," "synergize," "utilize," "implement"
- "innovative," "cutting-edge," "best-in-class," "game-changer"
- "very," "quite," "somewhat"
- "journey" (uses specific arcs), "passionate about," "excited to announce"
- Any hashtags in body text
- "hustle" / "grind" (despite being a hard worker, never uses hustle culture vocabulary)

**Note on "really":** Used deliberately in Sample 07 ("You love tech. You really do.") as an emotional beat. Acceptable as empathy device, not as vague qualifier.

### Ending Patterns

| Pattern | Example | Use When |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Question + engagement emoji | "What do you think - curse or opportunity? (pointing down emoji)" | Want discussion |
| Bold declaration | "Smart move by Nike." | Case study |
| Direct command | "Sell the vision until the market literally forces you to build." | Teaching post |
| Summary restatement | "Advisors, Networking, Partners - make or break." | Framework post |
| Clean landing (no CTA) | "You don't need to try every tool. You need a system for deciding which ones are worth your time." | When the insight IS the ending |
| Tension question (either/or) | Hebrew post: two valid opposing positions as a question | Investment/strategy posts |

**Ending rules:** If the last line already delivers a clear insight, stop. Don't add a forced reframe. "That's it." can be a powerful ending on its own. When adding a question, make it specific and either/or. Never generic "What do you think?"

### Punctuation Fingerprint

- Single dash with space (` - `) for parenthetical: "trust, and social proof - gone in one algorithm update"
- Dash-without-space before turns: "This wasn't a mistake- WHOOP knew the rules" / "Instead-"
- Colon as reveal device: "Their message was clear:" / "My response was simple:"
- Period-separated short phrases for rhythm: "It was ugly. But it worked." / "No doubt."
- NEVER em dash, NEVER double dash (--)
- NEVER curly/smart quotes. Always straight quotes (" ') only.

### Pronoun Precision

- "I" for personal stories and opinions: "I made what many called a reckless decision"
- "we" for company decisions: "Did we switch? No." / "Let's focus on what's next."
- "the team" to separate self from org: "when the team showed me the update"
- "you" for direct reader address: "You love tech. You really do."
- Never "one" or passive constructions for personal stories

### "It" as Forward-Pull Device

Starts sentences with vague "It" that makes reader lean in: "It wasn't cheap" / "It's just choosing to pay" / "It was ugly. But it worked." Creates momentum because reader needs the next line to understand what "it" refers to.

### Casual Profanity (Controlled)

- "Hell no." (Nike post) and "avoid the BS" - conviction, not aggression
- Rare: 1-2 per 9 samples. Only when emphasis demands it.

### Dialogue as Structural Pivot

Dialogue carries the narrative, not just decorates it:
- Coffee shop post pivots on: "How many customers have paid for this?"
- Shopify post: quoted reaction IS the lesson: "Doesn't matter. Let's focus on what's next."
- Hebrew post: someone else's words as the hook

### Hebrew Content Patterns

Hebrew posts follow the same structure with these additions:
- Uses business English terms naturally: "Tier-1," "Gravity," "due diligence," "signal"
- More emotionally direct than English: explicit emotional vocabulary ("my ego was a bit hurt," "I was offended")
- Vivid locally resonant analogies
- Audience-segmented takeaways: breaks insights by reader type
- Closes with tension question that has no easy answer

## Tone Rule: Eye-Level, Never Condescending (CRITICAL)

**Lior explicitly stated: the tone must be at eye-level, never condescending.**

**What condescending sounds like (AVOID):**
- "Not because I have more time than you. Because I treat myself like I treat my product." implies reader is lazy
- Isolated short sentences that lecture: "Things never calm down." on its own line feels like a guru pronouncement
- Dramatically staged closing questions

**What eye-level sounds like (DO THIS):**
- "It's not because I'm working harder than everyone else, but because I treat myself like I treat my product." The "but because" connects instead of contrasts
- Punch lines absorbed into the paragraph they belong to, a shared observation, not a pronouncement
- Closing question flowing naturally from the content, not staged as a mic-drop moment
- "most people I know" includes himself in the group

**The Rule:** If a sentence could be read as "I'm better than you and here's why," rewrite it. Lior shares what works FOR HIM. He doesn't imply the reader is failing.

## Owner Editing Patterns (How Lior Refines Drafts)

**What Lior cuts from system drafts:**
- Closing "mic drop" lines that feel forced or preachy
- Bold formatting on numbered list headers, prefers inline flow
- Sentences that over-explain what the reader already understood
- "I've been there" jumps straight to the solution
- Extra white space, prefers tighter flow
- Short punchy sentences that sound condescending alone on a line
- Superiority framing ("Not because I have more time than you")

**What Lior keeps:**
- Empathy-first hooks ("You love tech. You really do.")
- Binary tension ("try everything and burn out, or ignore everything and fall behind")
- Rhetorical self-answers ("Did we switch? No.")
- "That's it." as standalone closer
- Reframe sentences ("The tool that works is always better than the tool that's trending")

**What Lior changes:**
- "I" to "we" in company examples ("Did we switch?" not "Did I switch?")
- Removes final tagline if the substance already landed
- Tightens multiple qualifiers to one
- Standalone punch lines get absorbed into paragraph flow
- Closing question glued to final step's paragraph, not given its own dramatic section
- "Not because I have more time than you" becomes "It's not because I'm working harder than everyone else, but because..."
