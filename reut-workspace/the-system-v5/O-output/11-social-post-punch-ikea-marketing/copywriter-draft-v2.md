### The Copy

How did IKEA turn a baby monkey into one of the best brand moments of 2026?

They didn't plan it. They recognized it.

Here's the story:

A 7-month-old macaque named Punch was abandoned by his mother at a zoo in Japan.

Zookeepers gave him a $20 IKEA plush orangutan as a comfort toy. He grabbed it and wouldn't let go.

30M+ views on TikTok. Global news coverage. Unprecedented crowds at the zoo.

But IKEA didn't just watch. They moved.

IKEA Japan's CEO personally visited the zoo and donated dozens of plushies.

IKEA Spain repurposed Punch's image in a catalog-style ad.

IKEA Hong Kong posted: "You can't adopt him. Buy this IKEA one instead."

And the line that tied it all together: "Sometimes family is who we find along the way."

No agency brief. No campaign timeline. Just real-time marketing done right.

Now here's the part most marketers miss:

This didn't go viral because of a monkey. It went viral because of a feeling.

An abandoned baby clinging to the only thing that gives him comfort. Every person who watched that video projected their own story onto it. Loneliness. Rejection. Finding something to hold onto.

That's not content. That's a mirror.

And IKEA understood that the moment wasn't about selling a toy. It was about aligning their brand with that emotion before anyone else could.

The best brands don't create feelings. They show up when those feelings already exist.
