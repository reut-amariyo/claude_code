### What's Good

1. **Structure matches WHOOP exactly.** Provocative question > "Here's the story:" > incident > brand response > escalation > POV. Proven format.

2. **The $20 detail is brilliant.** A $20 toy selling out in 3 countries with zero ad spend is the kind of contrast that makes people stop scrolling.

3. **Actual quotes from IKEA ads.** "Sometimes family is who we find along the way" and "You can't adopt him. Buy this IKEA one instead." Real details. Can't be faked.

4. **"Most companies would have posted a retweet and moved on."** Strong contrast. Shows what average brands do vs what IKEA did.

5. **"The best marketing isn't planned. It's recognized."** Clean, quotable closing. Reframe pattern. Will get shared.

---

### What Needs Work

1. **"From a marketing perspective, this is what separates brands that react from brands that wait for the next quarterly meeting to decide if a trend is 'on brand.'"** Too long. One of the longest sentences in the post. The "quarterly meeting" jab is good but the sentence needs tightening. Suggestion: "This is what separates brands that react from brands that schedule a meeting about reacting."

2. **The post could use one Lior personal touch.** The WHOOP post was pure analysis and worked at 220K impressions. But based on Post 10 learnings, owner might want to add a single line connecting this to his own experience - something like referencing how he handles real-time opportunities at AutoDS. Optional, depends on owner preference.

---

### Recommendation

**APPROVED for owner review.** Format matches the proven 220K-impression WHOOP structure. Story is timely, details are specific, ending is quotable.
