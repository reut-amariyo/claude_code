## Copy Delivery: LinkedIn Post v1

**Channel:** LinkedIn
**Goal:** External Brand Story - marketing analysis of IKEA's response to Punch the monkey going viral. Matching the WHOOP post format (220K+ impressions).

---

### The Copy

How does a $20 stuffed toy sell out in 3 countries without a single ad?

IKEA just gave a masterclass in reactive marketing.

Here's the story:

A 7-month-old baby monkey named Punch was abandoned by his mother at a zoo in Japan.

Zookeepers gave him a $20 IKEA plush orangutan as a comfort toy.

Punch grabbed it. Clung to it. Wouldn't let go.

The internet did the rest.

30M+ views on TikTok. Global news coverage. Unprecedented crowds lined up at the zoo just to see him.

But here's what makes this a marketing story:

IKEA didn't create this moment. They reacted to it.

Within days, IKEA Japan's CEO personally visited the zoo and donated dozens of plush toys.

Their ad said: "Sometimes family is who we find along the way."

IKEA Hong Kong ran their own version: "You can't adopt him. Buy this IKEA one instead."

The $20 Djungelskog orangutan sold out in Japan, the US, and South Korea.

No campaign. No influencer deal. No media buy.

Just a brand that moved fast, led with empathy, and let the story do the selling.

Most companies would have posted a retweet and moved on. IKEA turned a moment they didn't plan into a global brand event.

From a marketing perspective, this is what separates brands that react from brands that wait for the next quarterly meeting to decide if a trend is "on brand."

The best marketing isn't planned. It's recognized.
