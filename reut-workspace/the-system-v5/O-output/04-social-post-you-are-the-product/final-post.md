# Final Post: You Are The Product

**Status:** Published (Owner's final version)
**Platform:** LinkedIn
**Date:** 2026-02-15
**Version:** v2-final (Owner edited)

---

You spend weeks on your product roadmap. You obsess over every feature, every pricing model, every user flow. But when was the last time you put that energy into yourself? Most people I know treat their personal growth like a nice-to-have.
Something they'll get to "when things calm down." Things never calm down.
I manage multiple products and teams, work out 5 times a week, and still push myself to learn new skills.
It's not because I'm working harder than everyone else, but because I treat myself like I treat my product. Here's what that looks like in practice:
1. I roadmap my growth. Every quarter I set personal goals the same way I set product OKRs. What skill am I building? What habit am I fixing? If it's not on my calendar, it doesn't exist.
2. I debug myself first. When my focus drops or decisions slow down, I don't push harder. I check the basics. Sleep. Food. Exercise. Most performance problems aren't motivation problems. They're infrastructure problems.
3. I delegate ruthlessly. If someone can do it 70% as well as me, they're doing it. I stopped editing decks, reviewing minor expenses, and scheduling my own meetings a long time ago. That freed up hours I now spend on things only I can do. You'd never ship a product without a roadmap, testing, and iteration. Why are you running yourself without one?
