### The Copy

Every AI tool I use knows exactly who I am.

A year ago, I sat down and wrote one long prompt about myself. Not a bio. A full operating system.

How I think. How I write. How I lead. What I never say. My actual stories. My management rules. Even how I make decisions.

Now every email, every post, every investor update - I feed it that prompt first.

The result: AI sounds like me. Not like a robot pretending to be a CEO.

Most people open ChatGPT, type "write me a cold email," and wonder why it sounds like it was written by a stranger. It was.

I run 3 companies with 250+ employees. I write investor emails, team updates, strategy docs, LinkedIn posts. One prompt handles all of it because it captures the one thing no tool can guess: who I actually am.

Here's what's in mine:

1. Who I am and how I got here - my origin story, my roles, my businesses. Not a resume. The version I'd tell over coffee.

2. How I think and operate - my decision framework, management rules, leadership style. The patterns I repeat and the mistakes I've already made.

3. How I communicate - my tone, my vocabulary, the words I use and the words I never use. Platform-specific rules for LinkedIn, email, internal docs.

That's it. One document. Every AI tool gets it before I ask for anything.

The problem was never the tool. It was the input.

I put together a blank template based on the exact structure I use so you can build your own.

Comment "PROMPT" and I'll send it to you.

### Notes

- **Archetype:** Empathy-First Framework + Personal System (hybrid)
- **Hook type:** Transformation (implies a system)
- **Character count:** ~1,450 characters (within 1,200-1,600 sweet spot)
- **Key changes from v1/v2:**
  - "full operating system" replaces vague "prompt" framing - matches the Notion doc's depth
  - Framework now reflects real structure: identity/story, thinking/operating, communication - not the generic "background, voice rules, audience" from v1
  - Added "250+ employees" signposting (per LinkedIn skill rules)
  - Added "my management rules, even how I make decisions" - shows the prompt goes beyond just writing
  - Comment-to-unlock CTA using "PROMPT" keyword (per LinkedIn skill e-book framework)
  - Removed either/or closing question - replaced with lead magnet CTA for engagement
- **Sentence patterns used:** The Reframe ("The problem was never the tool. It was the input."), Proof-Through-Specifics (3 companies, 250+ employees), Direct Command ("Comment PROMPT"), Rule of 3 (3 framework sections)
- **Eye-level tone:** "Most people" is observational. Shows his system, doesn't lecture about why others are wrong.
- **Anti-AI rules:** No fluff, short paragraphs, no double dashes, no emojis, direct tone, signposting
