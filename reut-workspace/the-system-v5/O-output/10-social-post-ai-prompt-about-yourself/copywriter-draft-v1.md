## Copy Delivery: LinkedIn Post

**Channel:** LinkedIn
**Goal:** Position Lior as an AI authority - not as an AI influencer, but as a CEO who uses AI better than most because of one simple habit.

---

### The Copy

Every AI tool I use knows exactly who I am.

Not because I built something fancy. Because about a year ago, I sat down and wrote one prompt about myself.

My voice. My businesses. How I think. How I write. What I never say.

Now every email, every LinkedIn post, every investor update, every internal memo - I feed it that prompt first.

The result: AI sounds like me. Not like a robot pretending to be a CEO.

Here's what most people get wrong:

They open ChatGPT, type "write me a cold email," and wonder why the output sounds like it was written by a stranger.

It was. The AI doesn't know you. You never introduced yourself.

I run 3 companies. I write investor emails, team updates, LinkedIn posts, strategy docs. One prompt handles all of it because it captures the one thing no tool can guess: who I actually am.

Here's what I put in mine:

1. My background and what I do - not a bio, but how I'd describe myself to a friend over coffee.

2. My voice rules - short sentences, no fluff, no corporate jargon, specific numbers over vague claims. The words I use and the words I never use.

3. My audience - who I'm talking to, what they care about, and how they want to hear it.

That's it. Three sections. One document.

Every AI tool I touch gets it before I ask for anything.

Most founders I know are spending hours fixing AI output that was broken from the start. The problem was never the tool. It was the input.

Write the prompt once. Use it everywhere. Are you still giving AI a blank page, or have you already taught it who you are?

---

### Notes

- **Archetype:** Empathy-First Framework + Personal Confession hybrid
- **Hook type:** Transformation (implies a system others don't have)
- **Character count:** ~1,350 characters (within 1,200-1,600 sweet spot)
- **Sentence patterns used:** The Reframe ("The problem was never the tool. It was the input."), Proof-Through-Specifics (3 companies, investor emails, team updates), Direct Command ("Write the prompt once. Use it everywhere."), Rule of 3 (3 framework steps)
- **Ending pattern:** Either/or tension question
- **Time anchor:** "about a year ago"
- **Eye-level tone:** "Most founders I know" includes Lior in the group. Shares his system, doesn't lecture.
- **Anti-AI rules applied:** No fluff, one-sentence paragraphs, no double dashes, no emojis, direct tone, signposting ("I run 3 companies")
