# Strategist Brief: Writing a Prompt About Yourself

## The Trend & Friction

* **The Trend:** New AI tools launch daily in 2026. Everyone is using ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and dozens of niche tools for emails, posts, strategy docs. But almost nobody is telling AI *who they are* first. They jump straight to "write me a post" without giving AI the context it needs to sound like them.

* **The Friction:** Founders and entrepreneurs are getting generic, interchangeable AI output. Their emails sound like everyone else's emails. Their posts could have been written by any CEO. They're frustrated that AI "doesn't get them" - but they never taught it who they are. The result: AI saves time but kills authenticity. The deeper problem: people treat AI like a vending machine (input task, get output) instead of a collaborator that needs context.

* **Target Audience:** Entrepreneurs and Growth-Seekers (primary). Investors (secondary - they notice when a founder's communication is sharp and consistent).

---

## The Narrative Hook

* **Recommended Archetype:** Empathy-First Framework (Sample 07 pattern) + Personal Confession hybrid. Start by validating that everyone uses AI now. Then reveal the gap: they're using it wrong because they skipped step zero.

* **Hook Type:** Transformation hook. Before/after framing: "Every AI tool I use knows exactly who I am" - implies a system others don't have, creates curiosity.

* **The Angle:** Lior wrote a detailed prompt about himself - his voice, his business, his values, his writing style. He feeds it into every AI interaction: emails to investors, LinkedIn posts, internal docs, strategy memos. The result: AI output that sounds like him, not like a robot. This is the "step zero" most people skip. Lior doesn't just use AI - he trained it on himself first.

* **The "Gold" Detail:** Lior runs 3 companies (AutoDS, BuildYourStore.ai, CreateUGC.AI), manages 250+ employees, writes investor emails, LinkedIn posts, and internal communications. One prompt powers all of it. Specific and visual.

* **Lior Story Asset:** No single story from the 8 assets fits perfectly. This is a NEW story - Lior's current daily workflow with AI. The "realness" comes from specific use cases: investor emails, LinkedIn posts, team communications. The proof is in the specificity of what he uses it for across 3 companies.

---

## Instructions for Copywriter

* **Voice DNA patterns to use:**
  - Empathy hook opening ("You use AI. Everyone does." - validates, doesn't lecture)
  - The Reframe as core insight ("The tool isn't the problem. The input is.")
  - Conditional Conviction for the framework steps ("If AI doesn't know your voice, it can't speak in your voice.")
  - Proof-Through-Specifics: list what Lior actually uses AI for (emails, posts, strategy docs) across 3 companies. Don't claim "I'm good at AI." Show it.

* **Sentence architecture:** Story + Lesson structure (LinkedIn Skill Structure 1). Dense teaching paragraphs (Sample 09 style) for the framework section. Keep it in the 1,200-1,600 character sweet spot.

* **Ending pattern:** Either/or tension question. "Are you still giving AI a blank prompt, or have you taught it who you are?" Forces the reader to self-assess.

* **Watch out for:**
  - Don't sound like an AI guru. Lior is a CEO who happens to use AI well, not an AI influencer.
  - Don't use "leverage AI" or "optimize your workflow" - banned vocabulary.
  - Don't announce intent ("I want to talk about prompts"). Just talk about it.
  - Keep it eye-level. This is sharing a personal system, not teaching from above.
  - No "game-changer," no "cutting-edge," no "innovative."
  - Time anchor needed: when did Lior start doing this?
