## What Works Well

1. **Hook execution:** 3-stat stacking into "Not a single line of code" punch is strong. Follows the Shocking Stat hook pattern (Sample 04). Numbers are specific and visual.
2. **Transition fix:** Replacing "But here's the thing most people miss" (LinkedIn cliche) with "But the tech is never the real story" was the right call. Direct, no filler.
3. **AutoDS story is authentic:** "Zero automation. We sold the vision first." followed by "We needed the market to prove it with their credit cards." This is quintessential Lior - Reframe pattern, specific, and shows before telling.
4. **Pronoun precision:** "we" for company decisions (AutoDS story), "They" for Spotify. Correct per voice-dna Section 11.
5. **Punctuation correct:** Single dash with space in closing question. No em dashes, no double dashes. Straight quotes only.
6. **Ending pattern:** Question + 👇 matches Sample 04 ending. The question has genuine tension (engineers writing no code = provocative).
7. **"This is not the future. This is now."** - Power sentence, 7 words each. Punch-line pattern confirmed.

## What Needs Improvement

### 1. "Not a single line of code written by a human" - trim it

**Issue:** The owner's original said "not a single line of code." The copywriter added "written by a human" which weakens the punch. The shorter version is stronger and more Lior.

**Fix:** Change to: "Not a single line of code."

### 2. Closing universal principle line feels added-on

**Issue:** "The pattern is the same whether you're a startup or a $100B company: sell the vision, validate the demand, then build." This reads like an AI-generated summary line. Lior doesn't spell out universal principles - he lets the reader connect the two stories themselves. The Spotify story + AutoDS story already make the point. Spelling it out is over-explaining (Owner Editing Rule: cuts "sentences that over-explain what the reader already understood").

**Fix:** Remove the line entirely. Let "Only after that did we build the tech." flow directly into "Spotify didn't start with AI. They started with engineers who understood what needed to be built." The reader gets the parallel without being told.

### 3. "Spotify didn't start with AI" - needs sharper bridge

**Issue:** After removing the universal principle line, the transition from AutoDS back to Spotify needs to land harder. The current line is good but could be tighter.

**Fix:** Keep "Spotify didn't start with AI. They started with engineers who understood what needed to be built." - this works as the bridge AND the closing insight. It echoes the Reframe pattern: "[Thing you'd expect]. [Actual truth]."
