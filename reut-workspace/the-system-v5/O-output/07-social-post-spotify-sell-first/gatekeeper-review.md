## What Works Well

1. **Angle is fresh.** Post 06 led with stats and "vision before code." This one leads with the lesson ("sell first, build second") and uses Spotify as evidence. Different enough to not feel like a repeat.
2. **"with their credit cards, not their compliments"** - Strong Reframe. Concrete, quotable, uniquely Lior. This is the best line in the draft.
3. **Conditional Conviction ending** ("If they aren't trying to hand you money...") - Directly from Lior's own words (sample 03). Feels authentic, not AI-generated.
4. **Time anchoring** works. "7 years ago" and "since December" ground both stories.
5. **Closing question is specific** ("What are you building right now that you haven't sold yet?") - Actionable, not generic. Invites real answers.
6. **Pronoun precision is correct.** "we" for AutoDS, "they" for Spotify, "you" for reader.
7. **No banned words, no double dashes, no curly quotes, no hashtags in body.** Clean.

## What Needs Improvement

1. **"Everyone is talking about the tech. I want to talk about the pattern behind it."** - Two problems:
   - "I want to talk about" is weak. Lior doesn't announce what he's going to do. He does it. This is a "telling" sentence where Lior would just "show."
   - It echoes post 06's "But the tech is never the real story" - same idea, different words.
   - **Fix:** Cut these two lines entirely. The post works without them. After "All through AI." jump straight to "7 years ago, we launched..." The reader will feel the pivot naturally.

2. **"Here's what most founders get wrong: they build first and sell second."** - Borderline condescending.
   - "most founders get wrong" is a guru framing. Lior shares what works for HIM, not what others "get wrong."
   - **Fix:** Reframe as personal observation. Something like: "I see it A LOT. Months of development. Perfect features. Beautiful UI. Then they launch to silence." This uses Lior's "A LOT" vocabulary and stays observational.

3. **"Then they launch to silence."** - Good punch line, but it's on its own line after a paragraph. Per Owner Editing Patterns, standalone punch lines should be absorbed into paragraph flow.
   - **Fix:** Glue it to the previous paragraph: "Beautiful UI. And then they launch to silence."

4. **"Spotify didn't start with AI either. They started with engineers who understood what needed to be built. The AI just made it faster."** - Three sentences about Spotify after the AutoDS story breaks the momentum. The AutoDS story is the heart. Spotify should bridge back quickly, not get its own analysis block.
   - **Fix:** Compress to one sentence. "Spotify didn't start with AI. They started with the problem." Then move straight to the principle.

5. **Missing signposting.** Per LinkedIn skill rules, add real-world authority markers. The AutoDS story has no scale markers. Add something like "at AutoDS" with a mention, or reference the scale briefly.

## Revised Version (Suggested)

Spotify's co-CEO just said their top developers haven't written a single line of code since December.

They shipped 50+ features. Served 751 million users. All through AI.

7 years ago, we launched our Research tool at AutoDS. There was no code. Zero automation. We had a manual team behind the scenes finding products and delivering them by hand.

We sold the vision first. When the first users paid - with their credit cards, not their compliments - we knew it was real. Only then did we build the tech.

Spotify didn't start with AI. They started with the problem.

I see it A LOT. Months of development. Perfect features. Beautiful UI. And then they launch to silence.

Sell the vision until the market literally forces you to build the solution. If they aren't trying to hand you money for a product that doesn't exist yet, don't build it.

What are you building right now that you haven't sold yet? 👇
