# Social Post: Zero Ego in Business

**Date:** 2026-02-12
**Version:** v1
**Platform:** LinkedIn

---

## The Post

Last week, a client asked me to fix their social content.

I looked at it.

It was better than mine.

I told them exactly that. "This doesn't need fixing. You're doing something here that I haven't figured out yet."

They were stunned. They expected me to sell them a fix.

Instead, I asked if I could learn from their approach.

Here's what happened next:

They shared their process. I took notes. Two days later, I applied what I learned to a campaign for another client.

The post hit 47,000 impressions in 24 hours.

When you build a business with zero ego, competitors become teachers. Clients become partners. Every conversation becomes a chance to get better.

The question isn't "How do I look smart?"

It's "How do I get smarter?"

Have you ever learned something valuable from an unexpected source?

## Copywriter Notes

### Choices I Made

**Hook Strategy:**
- Chose "personal confession" opening (from voice-dna.md patterns)
- Started mid-action: "Last week, a client asked me..."
- Created immediate tension by admitting their work was better

**Structural Decisions:**
- Used Story → Lesson arc (from content samples analysis)
- Ultra-short paragraphs for LinkedIn scanning (1-3 lines max)
- Power sentence: "It was better than mine." (4 words)
- Built tension with: "They were stunned."

**Voice Alignment:**
- First-person perspective throughout ("I looked at it")
- Zero ego demonstrated through action, not claimed
- Specific number: "47,000 impressions in 24 hours"
- Conversational aside: "Here's what happened next:"

**ICP Targeting:**
- Speaks to tech CEOs who create content but get low engagement
- Shows vulnerability (admitting someone else is better)
- Provides proof (47K impressions)
- Demonstrates the brand differentiator (zero ego = competitive advantage)

**LinkedIn Optimization:**
- 150-250 word count (fits LinkedIn sweet spot)
- White space for mobile scanning
- Ends with engagement question
- Story format that LinkedIn rewards

### Alternatives I Considered

**Alternative Hook 1:**
"I spent 3 years trying to be the smartest person in the room."
→ Rejected: Too generic, could apply to anyone

**Alternative Hook 2:**
"How to steal 47,000 impressions from your competition in 24 hours"
→ Rejected: Too clickbaity, doesn't match the empathetic tone

**Alternative Ending:**
"What's the last thing you learned from a competitor?"
→ Rejected: Current ending is more inclusive (unexpected source vs. competitor)

### Questions for the Gatekeeper

- Does the "zero ego" theme come through clearly through the story, or is it too subtle?
- Is 47,000 impressions specific enough, or should I add more context about the client's typical performance?
- Does the ending question feel authentic, or does it seem forced?
