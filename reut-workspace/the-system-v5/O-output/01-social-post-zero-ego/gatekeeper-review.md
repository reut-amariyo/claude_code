## What Could Be Even Better (Minor Notes)

**Tension Build:**
- The line "They were stunned" could be shown rather than told
- Consider: "Silence on the Zoom call." or "Long pause. Then: 'Wait, what?'"
- Current version works, but showing the stunned reaction would be stronger

**Lesson Clarity:**
- The two-question ending is good, but slightly diffuses focus
- The second question ("How do I get smarter?") is the real insight
- Could work as standalone power line

**That said:** These are refinements, not blockers. The post is publication-ready as-is.

## Loop Notes

**Patterns Worth Logging:**
1. **"Show stunned" technique:** When describing reactions, showing beats telling (e.g., "Silence on the call" vs "They were stunned")
2. **Dual-question endings:** Two questions can work, but single focused question often stronger
3. **Admission hooks:** Starting with "I was wrong" or "They were better" creates immediate trust + tension

**What Worked Exceptionally Well:**
- Zero ego demonstrated through narrative (not claimed as a trait)
- Specific number placement: 47,000 impressions serves as both proof and teaching moment
- Conversational aside timing: "Here's what happened next:" creates natural transition

**For M-memory/learning-log.md:**
