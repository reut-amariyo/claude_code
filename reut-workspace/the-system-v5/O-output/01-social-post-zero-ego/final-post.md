# Final Post: Zero Ego in Business

**Status:** Approved for publishing
**Platform:** LinkedIn
**Approval date:** 2026-02-12

---

Last week, a client asked me to fix their social content.

I looked at it.

It was better than mine.

I told them exactly that. "This doesn't need fixing. You're doing something here that I haven't figured out yet."

They were stunned. They expected me to sell them a fix.

Instead, I asked if I could learn from their approach.

Here's what happened next:

They shared their process. I took notes. Two days later, I applied what I learned to a campaign for another client.

The post hit 47,000 impressions in 24 hours.

When you build a business with zero ego, competitors become teachers. Clients become partners. Every conversation becomes a chance to get better.

The question isn't "How do I look smart?"

It's "How do I get smarter?"

Have you ever learned something valuable from an unexpected source?
