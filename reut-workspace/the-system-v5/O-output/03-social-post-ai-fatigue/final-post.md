# Final Post: AI Fatigue

**Status:** Published (Owner's final version)
**Platform:** LinkedIn
**Date:** 2026-02-12
**Version:** v3-final (Owner edited)

---

You love tech. You really do.
But somewhere between the 4th AI tool launch this week and your actual to-do list, you stopped keeping up.
You're not alone.
Most of the people I talk to feel the same.
They genuinely care about staying ahead, but when it comes to getting work done, there's just no bandwidth left to evaluate every new tool that drops.
So they do one of two things: try everything and burn out, or ignore everything and fall behind.
Here's what I do to stay sharp without drowning in noise:
1. Start from the need, not the tool. Don't ask "what AI tools are out there." Ask "What's the one thing slowing me down the most right now?" Find the pain first. Then go look for a solution. If you start from the tool, you'll end up solving problems you don't have.
2. Pick 2-3 people you trust. Ignore the rest. I follow a handful of people who actually test what they recommend. That's it. No more scrolling through "Top 50 AI Tools" lists at midnight. Find your 2-3 trusted sources and cut out everything else.
3. Stop chasing. Start reviewing. We automated one reporting workflow 8 months ago. Saved us 6 hours a week. Last month, a better tool was launched. Did we switch? No. Because the tool that works is always better than the tool that's trending. Once a quarter, I review what I use and decide if anything needs to change.
That's it.
You don't need to try every tool. You need a system for deciding which ones are worth your time.
