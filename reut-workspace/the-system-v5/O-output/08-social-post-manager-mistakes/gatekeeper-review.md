## Status: APPROVED (with 3 minor suggestions)

## What Works Well

1. **Hook is clean and specific.** "The biggest management mistake I made for years: doing everything myself." - Personal confession hook, colon as reveal, one line. Passes all 8 LinkedIn hook principles (benefit, transformation, ease, specific, polarizing, short, clear).

2. **"I told myself... I was wrong on both counts."** Vulnerability before credibility. Sets up the entire post as a confession, not a lecture. Eye-level tone from the start.

3. **The 3 "I stopped..." steps are the best part of this draft.** Each step has: what I stopped + what happened + an honest admission. The 70% rule ("If someone can do it 70% as well as me, they're doing it.") is directly from Lior's words in Sample 09. Authentic.

4. **"Some of them made better decisions than I would have. That was hard to admit. But it was true."** This is peak Lior voice. Self-aware aside + vulnerability + restraint. Eye-level. Would pass the "could this be read as 'I'm better than you'?" test.

5. **Closing reframe from Sample 02.** "Doing it all isn't saving money. It's just choosing to pay with your time instead of your bank account." Proven line. Quotable.

6. **Signposting is natural.** "250+ employees," "three companies," "15+ hours a week" - authority markers woven into the narrative, not flexed.

7. **Closing question is specific and actionable.** "What's the one thing you're still doing that someone else should be doing?" Invites real answers.

8. **No voice violations.** No banned words, no double dashes, no em dashes, no curly quotes, no hashtags. "We" for company, "I" for personal. Clean.

## Minor Suggestions (Not Blockers)

1. **"I thought that was hustle. It was actually the thing slowing us down."**
   - Using "hustle" even to reject it feels risky. Per vocabulary fingerprint: Lior never uses "hustle" or "grind." The subversion is clever but breaks a consistent pattern across 9 samples.
   - **Suggestion:** Replace with: "I thought that was what founders do. It was actually the thing slowing us down." Keeps the same structure, avoids the word entirely.

2. **Step 3 density.** "Running three companies with 250+ employees doesn't work if I'm reviewing someone's slide deck." This is the strongest line in step 3, but it's buried in the middle of the paragraph.
   - **Suggestion:** Lead with it: "Running three companies with 250+ employees doesn't work if I'm reviewing someone's slide deck. I stopped confusing being busy with being useful. My job became: set the vision, remove blockers, and get out of the way."

3. **Time anchoring is weak.** The post says "for years" in the hook but doesn't anchor when things changed. Per voice-dna structural rules, almost every post includes a specific time reference.
   - **Suggestion:** Add time anchor to the AutoDS context. "When I started AutoDS 10 years ago, I was the product manager, the customer support, the marketer, and the developer."
