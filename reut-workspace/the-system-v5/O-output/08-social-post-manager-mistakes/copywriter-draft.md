# Social Post: Manager Mistakes - The Biggest One I Made

**Date:** 2026-02-17
**Version:** v1
**Platform:** LinkedIn

---

## The Post

The biggest management mistake I made for years: doing everything myself.

I told myself I was saving money. I told myself nobody could do it as well as me. I was wrong on both counts.

When I started AutoDS, I was the product manager, the customer support, the marketer, and the developer. At the same time. I thought that was hustle. It was actually the thing slowing us down.

Here's what changed when I stopped:

1. I stopped editing decks, reviewing minor expenses, and scheduling my own meetings. If someone can do it 70% as well as me, they're doing it. That rule alone freed up 15+ hours a week.

2. I stopped being the bottleneck on every decision. I hired people I trust and gave them space to make mistakes. Some of them made better decisions than I would have. That was hard to admit. But it was true.

3. I stopped confusing being busy with being useful. Running three companies with 250+ employees doesn't work if I'm reviewing someone's slide deck. My job became: set the vision, remove blockers, and get out of the way.

Doing it all isn't saving money. It's just choosing to pay with your time instead of your bank account.

What's the one thing you're still doing that someone else should be doing? 👇

## Copywriter Notes

### Angle & Archetype
- **Archetype:** Personal Confession + Framework (hybrid of Sample 02 "Advisors" + Sample 09 "You Are The Product")
- **Angle:** Lior's own biggest management mistake (doing everything himself) → 3 specific things he stopped doing → the reframe
- **ICP targeting:** Entrepreneurs (stop doing everything), Growth-Seekers (delegation framework with the 70% rule), Investors (see a scalable operator who knows when to let go)

### Choices I Made

**Hook:** "The biggest management mistake I made for years: doing everything myself."
- Personal confession hook (Sample 02 pattern)
- Colon as reveal device (punctuation fingerprint)
- One-liner, immediately specific
- Hits the ICP pain directly: fear of letting go, fear of delegating

**"I told myself... I was wrong on both counts."**
- Vulnerability before credibility (emotional texture pattern)
- "both counts" is concrete, not vague

**"I thought that was hustle. It was actually the thing slowing us down."**
- Reframe pattern. Also deliberately uses "hustle" once to subvert it (not as a positive, but as the mistake). This is the only time Lior would use "hustle" because he's rejecting it, not celebrating it.

**Rule of 3 framework:** Three "I stopped..." confessions
- Uses the "I stopped..." Confession pattern (signature sentence #7)
- Each step: what I stopped + what happened + the honest admission
- "If someone can do it 70% as well as me, they're doing it." - Direct from Lior's own words (sample 09)
- "Some of them made better decisions than I would have. That was hard to admit. But it was true." - Self-aware aside + vulnerability
- "15+ hours a week" - Specific, visualizable number (signposting)
- "250+ employees" and "three companies" - Authority signposting without bragging

**Closing reframe:** "Doing it all isn't saving money. It's just choosing to pay with your time instead of your bank account."
- Direct from Sample 02 (Advisors post). This is Lior's own line, proven and quotable.

**Closing question:** "What's the one thing you're still doing that someone else should be doing?"
- Specific, actionable, invites real answers (not generic "What do you think?")
- Points emoji for engagement

### What I Avoided
- "Hustle" and "grind" as positive vocabulary (used "hustle" once, to reject it)
- Generic management advice ("delegate more," "trust your team")
- Condescending framing ("most founders get this wrong")
- Standalone punch lines on their own line
- Double dashes, em dashes, curly quotes
- "Leverage," "optimize," or any banned words

### Structural Decisions
- Dense paragraphs for the 3 steps (per Sample 09 trend toward density, learning log confirms)
- Each numbered step is a full inline paragraph, not a bullet fragment
- Closing reframe is absorbed into flow (not staged as mic-drop)
- Closing question glued to the reframe paragraph
