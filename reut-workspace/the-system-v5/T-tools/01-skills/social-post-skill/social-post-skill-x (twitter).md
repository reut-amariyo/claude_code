# X Post Skill

Create X posts that stop the scroll and start conversations. X-specific.

## When to Use

- Writing X posts (text, polls, article hooks)

- Creating thought leadership content for a professional audience

## 1. The 2026 Algorithm Logic (Essential)

You must write with the understanding that the X algorithm has shifted from "Early Engagement" to "AI-Content Matching":

- **Content > Timing:** Post timing and "Reply Guy" strategies are secondary because the AI reads the content to determine the audience.

- **The 24-Hour Burn:** Posts are now pushed for up to 24 hours. Write for "Dwell Time" (keeping people reading) rather than just a quick click.

- **Visual Bias:** Always suggest or incorporate a media element (screenshot, UI, or photo). Media can increase impressions by 5x-10x even if engagement is the same.

- **Authentic Journey:** Prioritize progress, setup updates, and "wins" over generic advice. People want the person, not just the opinion.

## 2. Character Count Strategy

| **Post Type** | **Length** | **Why it works** |
|---|---|---|
| **The Banger** | 40-80 chars | Ultra-short, shareable, screenshot-worthy. High repost rate. |
| **The Hot Take** | 100-200 chars | Bold opinion with just enough context. Triggers replies. |
| **The Story Tweet** | 200-280 chars | Full tweet. Personal moment + one insight. Maximizes dwell time. |
| **The Thread Opener** | 200-280 chars | Sets up a framework or story. Must end with tension to pull into thread. |
| **The Poll** | 100-150 chars + options | Question with 2-4 options. Drives engagement through voting. |

**Key:** X rewards brevity. If you can say it in 10 words, don't use 30.

## 3. Formatting & Style Constraints

- **The Punchline Filter:** Condense 200+ word LinkedIn posts into <50-word "Bangers" whenever possible. Every word must earn its place.

- **One Idea Per Post:** Never dilute the message. One punchy insight per tweet.

- **No Fluff:** Immediate start. No "In a world," "I'm excited," or "Here is why."

- **Structure:** Use single-line paragraphs. Use white space to increase dwell time.

- **Visuals:** If the content mentions a result, suggest: "Attach a screenshot of [Specific Metric/UI]."

- **No Hashtags in Body:** Hashtags kill reach on X. Zero hashtags unless it's a trending topic worth riding.

- **No Double Dashes:** Use periods or line breaks instead.

- **No Rocket Emoji:** It looks like spam.

## 4. Post Archetypes for Lior

### A. The "Banger" (Contrarian/Punchline)

- **Hook:** A bold, polarizing statement.

- **Body:** A short, logical teardown of the "old way."

- **Punchline:** The new truth.

- **Example:** "LeBron James: 15,000+ misses. Kevin Durant: 9,200+ misses. Deni Avdija: 2,200+ misses. These legends stepped onto the court knowing they'd add more misses to their record. But here's the thing: nobody in the arena will be counting. It's time to grant yourself the same grace. Stop keeping score of your misses. Start playing."

### B. The "Build in Public" Snapshot

- **Hook:** A specific milestone or "in the trenches" moment.

- **Body:** 2-3 bullet points of the tech stack or the "how."

- **Visual:** Suggest a screenshot of the dashboard or code.

- **Example:** "Just hit [Number] users. The stack: [Tool A], [Tool B], [Tool C]. Simple beats scalable until you actually have to scale."

### C. The "Value Thread" (For complex articles)

- **Tweet 1:** Massive outcome + "The Framework."

- **Tweets 2-5:** Tactical steps with zero fluff.

- **Final Tweet:** One high-value takeaway + Question for engagement.

### D. The "Reframe" (Signature Lior Move)

- **Hook:** Take something everyone thinks they understand.

- **Flip:** Show why the common understanding is wrong.

- **Land:** One sentence that reframes the entire concept.

- **Example:** "Pricing isn't about what your product is worth. It's about what your customer's problem costs them."

### E. The "Poll Play"

- **Setup:** A question that forces people to pick a side.

- **Options:** 2-4 choices. Make one slightly controversial.

- **Follow-up:** Quote-tweet the results with your actual take.

- **Example:** "What kills more startups? A) Bad product B) Bad pricing C) Bad timing D) Bad founders"

### F. The "Data Drop"

- **Hook:** One specific number that shocks.

- **Context:** 1-2 sentences explaining why it matters.

- **Takeaway:** What the reader should do with this information.

- **Example:** "AutoDS processes $150M+ in annual GMV. The number one thing that got us here wasn't the tech. It was saying no to 90% of feature requests."

## 5. Thread Structure

### The 5-Tweet Thread Template

```
Tweet 1 (Hook): Massive outcome or bold claim + "Here's the framework:"
   -> Must stand alone as a great tweet even without the thread

Tweet 2: Step 1 or Context. Set up the problem.

Tweet 3: The turning point. What actually changed.

Tweet 4: The tactical "how." Specific, actionable.

Tweet 5: The takeaway + engagement question.
```

### Thread Rules

- Tweet 1 must work as a standalone post
- Each tweet should be valuable on its own
- Don't number tweets (1/7, 2/7) - it kills engagement
- End with a question, not a summary
- Pin the thread after posting

## 7. X-Specific Engagement Rules

- **Reply to replies:** First 30 minutes after posting are critical. Reply to every comment.
- **Quote-tweet > Retweet:** Always add your take when sharing.
- **Bookmark bait:** Posts people save tend to get pushed harder. Frameworks and lists get bookmarked.
- **No links in tweets:** Links kill reach. Put links in the reply.
- **Pin your best performer:** Rotate your pinned tweet weekly.
- **Polls drive votes, not just views:** Polls get 2-3x the engagement of regular posts.
